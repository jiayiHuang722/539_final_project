ARTISTS

Louis Armstrong

1901-1971

Often hailed as the father of jazz, Louis Armstrong was a pioneering trumpeter and vocalist who played a key role in developing the genre into a fine art.

Duke Ellington

1899-1974

Duke Ellington, a master composer, pianist, and bandleader, elevated the big band sound to new artistic heights with his sophisticated compositions and orchestrations.

Billie Holiday

1915-1959

Known for her evocative voice and unique phrasing, Billie Holiday remains one of the most influential jazz vocalists, who brought deep emotion and nuance to the music she performed.

Miles Davis

1926-1991

A trumpet virtuoso and visionary composer, Miles Davis was instrumental in the evolution of jazz, from bebop through cool jazz and fusion.

John Coltrane

1926-1967

John Coltrane was a profound and innovative tenor saxophonist whose work in modal and free jazz has forever shaped the genre.

Ella Fitzgerald

1918-1996

Dubbed "The First Lady of Song," Ella Fitzgerald was known for her pure tone, impeccable diction, phrasing, timing, and a "horn-like" improvisational ability, particularly in her scat singing.

Charlie Parker

1920-1955

Charlie Parker was a legendary alto saxophonist who, as a leading figure in the development of bebop, introduced revolutionary harmonic ideas into jazz.

Thelonious Monk

1926-1991

With his unorthodox approach to the piano and distinctive compositional style, Thelonious Monk was a pivotal figure in the bebop movement and one of the most unique voices in jazz history.
